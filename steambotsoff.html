About STEAMbots

STEAMbots Program

Started by two high school students of Gretchen Whitney High School, STEAMbots is a program dedicated to spreading STEAM throughout our community. At a STEAMbots meet, students are able to interact with others from 2nd to 6th grade. Through specially designed activities and discussions, students are able to see the impact of STEAM in our live and the significance it holds. Our STEAMbots gather once a month in the Cerritos Library's Skyline Room (see contact for more information)!Every STEAMbot carries the core values of a STEAMbot: Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, Mathematics, Bravery, Organization, and Tenacity. We hope to see these values everywhere we walk!

Our Mission

STEAMbots strives to teach the wonders of STEAM with fun and challenging activites while building strong relationships between student to student. Our goal is to make STEAM an active part of our lives every minute of every day. We ultimately hope that STEAMbots opens students' eyes to future careers in STEAM, which is currently underlooked by many. STEAMing up the world will unfog our futures!

S for Science: March 26, 2019

In this lesson, students will learn what the scientific method is and how to apply it into their daily lives.

Section I

We will begin the class period by asking whether the students know what the scientific method is through the following question: Every scientists uses the scientific method... but what is it? After receiving responses from the students, we will explain the steps of the scientific method and a brief significance of each step. We will check to see the if the students understand by having them fill out a worksheet with a diagram of the scientific method.

Activity

The students will be told the following scenario: You are a owner of the most popular boat company in ages. Your boats are so great, that the President has asked you to build him a yacht that will host a grand party for his birthday with many people. He wants to invite as many people as possible. He has asked you to model your boat with the following ​materials: 2 sheets of foil, 3 popsicle sticks, and masking tape​​. The boat will be placed in a tub of water. Your task is to put as many people (M&Ms) into the boat as possible without the inside of the boat getting wet or the boat sinking. Students will be given 35 minutes to create a boat. During this time, they may test their boat as much as it is needed. They are to use the scientific method while building their boat. Assistance will be given as needed.

Section II

After the students are finished with the activity, we will explain to them that like this, the scientific method is used to create much of the technology we see today. For example, the first light bulb was created by Edison after much trial and error, as is practiced in the scientific method. We will each student to give examples of where they could use the scientific method in their lives.
